ASSAY INFORMATION, 'SARS-CoV-2 Swab Assay, RT-qPCR (LuNER)': The
Innovative Genomics Institute (IGI) SARS-CoV-2 LuNER Single and
Pooled Swab Assay is a real-time PCR (RT-PCR) test intended for the
qualitative detection of nucleic acid from the SARS-CoV-2 genome in
swab specimens from individuals infected with SARS-CoV-2. Testing is
limited to the IGI Clinical Laboratory, a Clinical Laboratory
Improvement Amendments (CLIA) certified high-complexity laboratory.
This test was developed, and its performance characteristics
determined, by IGI Clinical Laboratory. It has not been cleared or
approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. This test is
intended to be performed on respiratory swab specimens collected from
individuals who meet Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
clinical and/or epidemiological criteria for COVID-19 testing. CDC
COVID-19 criteria for testing on human specimens are available at
CDC's webpage Information for Healthcare Professionals: Coronavirus
Disease 2019 (COVID-19)(https://www.cdc.gov/
coronavirus/2019-ncov/hcp/index.html). If requested by public health
authorities, specimens will be sent for additional testing.
Laboratory test results should always be considered in the context of
clinical observations and epidemiological data in making a final
diagnosis and patient management decisions. Please see
https://www.fda.gov/media/136600/download for additional information
about interpreting results from RT-PCR based laboratory developed
tests, such as this one. A Positive result indicates that RNA from
SARS-CoV-2 (formerly 2019-nCoV) was detected, and the patient is
considered infected with the virus and presumed to be contagious. A
Negative test result for this test means that SARS-CoV-2 RNA was not
detected in the specimen. A negative result does not rule out the
possibility of COVID-19 and should not be used as the sole basis for
treatment or patient management decisions. If COVID-19 is still
suspected, based on exposure history together with other clinical
findings, re-testing should be considered in consultation with public
health authorities. A Specimen Insufficient result means the human
RNA control target was not detected, or the sample could not be
tested for other reasons. Additional sample collection may be
considered. PERFORMING SITE: INNOVATIVE GENOMICS INSTITUTE, 2151
